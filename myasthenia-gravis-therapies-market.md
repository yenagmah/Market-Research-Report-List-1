<p><p>The Myasthenia Gravis therapies market encompasses various types based on the condition's presentation. Ocular Myasthenia Gravis primarily affects eye muscles, leading to drooping eyelids and double vision. Congenital Myasthenia Gravis is inherited and observed from birth, characterized by muscle weakness. Generalized Myasthenia Gravis involves widespread muscle weakness affecting daily activities. Transient Myasthenia Gravis occurs temporarily, often postpartum or in association with certain infections. Other categories may include rarer forms or atypical presentations, requiring tailored therapeutic approaches.</p></p>
