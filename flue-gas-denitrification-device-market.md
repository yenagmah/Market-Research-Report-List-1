<p><p>The Flue Gas Denitrification Device market comprises two primary types: dry and wet flue gas denitrification devices. </p><p>Dry flue gas denitrification devices utilize solid reagents, such as activated carbon or zeolites, to adsorb nitrogen oxides, making them suitable for industrial applications with high temp operations. </p><p>In contrast, wet flue gas denitrification devices use liquid reagents, typically aqueous solutions, to chemically react with nitrogen oxides, effectively removing pollutants and often used in power plants where the gas can be easily scrubbed.</p></p>
