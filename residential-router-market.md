<p><p>The residential router market can be primarily divided into two types: wireless routers and wired routers. Wireless routers use Wi-Fi technology to provide internet connectivity without physical cables, enabling mobility and connectivity for multiple devices simultaneously. They are ideal for environments where devices are distributed throughout a home. In contrast, wired routers establish internet connections through Ethernet cables, offering more stable and secure connections, suitable for devices requiring consistent bandwidth, like gaming consoles or desktop computers. Both types cater to different user needs.</p></p>
