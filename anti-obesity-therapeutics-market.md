<p><p>The anti-obesity therapeutics market is primarily divided into centrally acting and peripherally acting anti-obesity drugs. Centrally acting drugs target neurotransmitters in the brain to suppress appetite and enhance satiety, thus facilitating weight loss. These include medications that modulate brain chemistry to reduce hunger signals. In contrast, peripherally acting drugs focus on the gastrointestinal system, inhibiting fat absorption or altering nutrient metabolism. Both categories work through distinct mechanisms, providing diverse options for obesity management and treatment.</p></p>
