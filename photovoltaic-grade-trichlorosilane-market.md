<p><p>The Photovoltaic Grade Trichlorosilane market primarily includes two production methods: the Direct Chlorination (DC) Process and the Hydrochlorination (HC) Process. The DC Process involves directly reacting silicon with chlorine gas to produce trichlorosilane, which is efficient and economical. In contrast, the HC Process utilizes metallurgical-grade silicon reacted with hydrogen chloride, often considered a more traditional method that can offer higher purity levels. Both processes cater to the increasing demand for high-quality trichlorosilane used in solar cell manufacturing.</p></p>
